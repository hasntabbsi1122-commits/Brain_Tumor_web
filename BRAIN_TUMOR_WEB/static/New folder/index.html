Overview

Understanding Brain Tumors

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells within the brain. These growths can disrupt normal brain function and require timely diagnosis.

What is a Brain Tumor?

Brain tumors are masses of abnormal cells that grow uncontrollably. They may be benign or malignant, both of which can have serious health implications.

Common Causes

  • Genetic mutations or inherited conditions
  • Exposure to radiation or harmful chemicals
  • Weakened immune system
  • Abnormal cell growth due to DNA damage

Role of AI in Detection

Deep learning models — particularly CNNs — analyze MRI scans to detect tumors with high precision, supporting faster and more accurate diagnoses.

Symptoms and Treatment

Early identification of symptoms significantly improves treatment outcomes.

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent or severe headaches
  • Vision or hearing disturbances
  • Seizures or coordination issues
  • Speech or memory difficulties
  • Behavioral or personality changes
  • Unexplained nausea or vomiting

Treatment Options

  • Surgical intervention
  • Radiation therapy
  • Chemotherapy
  • Targeted drug therapy
  • Rehabilitation therapies
  • Immunotherapy
